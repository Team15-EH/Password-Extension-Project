There are a variety of ways to make your password more secure.

One practice heralded by the National Cyber Security Centre is to find three random words from a dictionary and combine them to create a long string of random characters. This approach is appropriately titled the "Three Random Words" approach.

This approach aims to make it more difficult to break password's via brute force by both increasing their length and avoiding the useage of single word passwords which can be easily cracked by Dictionary attacks. Additionally this approach takes human memory into account by making passwords easier to remember than simply a random combination of characters.

Here are some general tips to increase your password's security.

>It is extremely dangerous to use easily accessible information about yourself.

>The longer your password the better as this will make it more difficult for brute force attacks to be successful

>Use a variety of numbers and special characters to stop basic wordlists.

>Each of these tips can be used in conunction with the three random words approach to create a secure password.

If you wish to make your password security more convenient or even stronger these tips will help!

Make use of a good password manager.

Ensure that two factor authentication is turned on and set up correctly.
